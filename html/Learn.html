What are cryptocurrencies


A cryptocurrency is a type of digital money or a virtual currency created from code. They function autonomously, outside of traditional banking and government systems, unlike the current traditional currency the government is then theoretically unable to interfere or manipulate them. Cryptocurrencies are created created using cryptography or a computer code and they are offering Transparency, decentralisation, immutability based on the Blockchain
Cryptocurrencies are wholly digital, so there is no physical coin or bill connected to the crypto you own. Instead, owners hold cryptocurrency in a digital wallet, and buy or sell through an online exchange.

Different types of crypto fall into 4 categories:


  1. Coins, which can include Bitcoin and altcoins (non-Bitcoin cryptocurrencies).Coins, like Bitcoin and Litecoin, are built on their own blockchain and are a store of value, meant to be used as money. The term "altcoin" is used to describe any blockchain-based cryptocurrency that isn't Bitcoin.
  2. Tokens, which are programmable assets that live within the blockchain of a given platform. Tokens are built on top of an existing blockchain. They can be used to represent other things of value, like points, coins, or even a piece of art. Basic Attention Token, or BAT, is one type of token.
  3. A meme or a joke coin. These were made just for fun, and now they are worth millions of dollars. For example, the name Dogelon Mars (ELON) was made up as a joke. It is supposed to make "InterPlanetary Money Transactions" easier when it is possible. Coinmarketcap.com says it has a market value of more than $500 million as of December 13.

The value of cryptocurrencies


The value of cryptocurrencies used the law of supply and demand which is an economic study that explains the link between the supply of an item or service and the demand for it in order to assess the impact on its price. The theory states the price variations of everything that may be traded on a market.
When a coin is in low supply or there is a significant demand for it, the price rises. Those who want to purchase it are eager to compete by giving ever-increasing prices. Alternatively, if there is an oversupply of a cryptocurrency and there is no demand for it, the price falls.
Generally , the rule of supply and demand states that when demand for something increases, providers will produce more of it. Manufacturers are eager to increase output in order to sell bigger amounts and profit from increased sales. But it is difficult with most cryptocurrencies for two simple reasons: they have a limited supply and are dispersed.
